22.5. Emily Short - Report rules not as flexible as I am used to

Reported by: Emily Short / Date: 3 November 2005

Nature: The source looks legal, but generates Problem messages

Build: 3C15

Platform: Mac OS X

Code example:

Mood is a kind of value. The moods are hostile, curious, friendly. A person has a mood. A person is usually curious.
A thing can be explored or unexplored. A thing is always unexplored.

Playing with is an action applying to one thing.

Carry out someone trying playing with something: now the noun is explored.

Report someone trying playing with something:
    say "[The person asked] pokes at [the noun] in a bored manner.";

Report someone hostile trying playing with something fixed in place:
    say "[The person asked] bangs its head against [the noun] repeatedly." instead.

Report someone hostile trying playing with something fixed in place for the first time:
    say "[The person asked] bangs its head against [the noun], raising a bit of a racket.

'I hope everything is all right in there,' Delores shouts from the other room.

'Swell!' you assure her." instead.

Office on Floor Fifty-One is a room. "Your office. It's cramped, what with the desk and the file drawers, but it's got a great view down Fifth Avenue."

What goes wrong:

Problem. You wrote 'Report someone hostile trying playing with something fixed in place' , which seems to introduce a rule, but the circumstances for the rule to apply ('someone hostile trying playing with something fixed in place') did not make sense.

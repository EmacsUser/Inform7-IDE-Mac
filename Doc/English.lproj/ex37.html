19.11. Implications

Extensions often need to define kinds, which we want to make as helpful as possible for the user. In particular, we want to make new instances of those kinds easy to create. An interesting example of this came up in Chapter 2, when clothing was introduced. For something to be a fully practical garment, worn by the player when play begins, it needs to be "worn", "wearable" and "initially carried": if the source forgets to specify the latter two properties, the results are mysterious and unfortunate.

The Standard Rules solve this problem by including the following line:

Something worn is usually wearable and initially carried.

Such a sentence is called an "implication", as it is in the form "Condition A implies Condition B". Inform never overrides certainties with mere implications, and is cautious about allowing them to build overly long chains of argument. This is to prevent the following kind of difficulty:

An open door is usually closed. A closed door is usually open.

Implications have not been mentioned up to now since they are only really needed by extensions, but also because they can be tricky, with unforeseen consequences. Handle with care.

Example (*). Rooms designated "outdoors" print a random atmospheric message every turn.

"Shadeless"

A room can be indoors or outdoors. A room is usually indoors.

Every turn when the location is outdoors:
    choose a random row in the Table of Environmental Events;
    say "[event entry][paragraph break]".

Table of Environmental Events
event
"The sun is really hot out here."
"My, the sun sure is warm."
"The heat is starting to get to you."
"A bird chirps."

No implications so far. But suppose we want to make a whole region outdoors without specifically saying so for every location in turn:

The Farewell Bend Cafe is a room. The Parking Lot is outside from the Farewell Bend Cafe. The Parking Lot is outdoors.

The Open Desert is a region. The Flat Place, the Hilly Place, and the Ridge are in the Open Desert. The Flat Place is west of the Parking Lot. The Hilly Place is west of the Flat Place. The Ridge is west of the Hilly Place. A room in the Open Desert is always outdoors.

Now the whole Desert will be baking hot.

Note that that "always outdoors" only means that all the rooms will be designated "outdoors" at the beginning of play. It doesn't prevent us changing that later, and indeed, out of pity for the player, we might want to provide a tent:

The tent is an enterable container carried by the player. Carry out entering the tent: now the location is indoors. Carry out exiting when the player is in the tent: now the location is outdoors.

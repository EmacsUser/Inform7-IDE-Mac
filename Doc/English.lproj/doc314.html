22.2. Emily Short - More logical fallacies

Reported by: Emily Short / Date: 5 November 2005

Nature: The source looks legal and produces a playable game, but the game does not do what it should

Build: 3C62

Platform: Mac OS X

Code example:

The Academy is a room. Aristotle is a man in the Academy. Aristotle is carrying an electric razor. The razor is a device.

The player wears a himation. The player carries an eel from the Copaic lake. The eel is edible.

Syllogism is a kind of value. The syllogisms are modus tollens and modus ponens.

Understand "use [syllogism]" as asserting. Understand "use syllogism of [syllogism] type" as asserting.

Understand "use [syllogism] on [someone]" as asserting it to. Understand "use [syllogism] in conversation with [someone]" as asserting it to. Understand "use syllogism of [syllogism] type on [someone]" as asserting it to. Understand "use on [someone] [syllogism]" as asserting it to (with nouns reversed).

Asserting is an action applying to one syllogism.

Asserting it to is an action applying to one syllogism and one thing.
Carry out asserting:
    do nothing.

Report asserting: say "You mumble something."

Instead of asserting modus ponens:
    say "'If P, then Q,' you assert. 'P! Therefore Q!'"

Instead of asserting modus tollens:
    say "'If P, then Q,' you assert. 'Not P! Therefore not Q!'"

Carry out asserting it to:
    say "You turn to [the second noun]. ";
    try asserting the syllogism understood instead.

What goes wrong:

>use modus ponens on aristotle

You turn to Aristotle. You may not name an object.

Comments:

I am not even certain whether this is really an I7 bug or just Something I Should Not Be Doing, TM. But other than this, my uber-complicated USE verb is doing extremely well, even with some scoping stuff thrown in for color, and "use no [text]", and all.

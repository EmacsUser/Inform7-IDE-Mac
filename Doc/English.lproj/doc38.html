Chapter 4: Actions

4.1. Giving instructions

This is a different sort of sentence to any seen so far:

When play begins: start the player at the Library; say "Welcome to Old Marston Grange, a country house cut off by fog."

This is not a passive statement of facts ("The Library is a room") but takes the form of an instruction to someone: that person is the computer, acting as our agent in talking with the player. It is an example of a "rule", the analogy being with the rules written inside the box-lid of an old board game, rather than rules in the scientific sense.

The sentence opens by identifying the circumstances in which the rule applies, leading up to the colon, and then gives a list of things to do, divided by semicolons except that the last ends with a full stop. In this case it's a list of two steps to follow, but rules vary in length from only one step to hundreds.

There's nothing to stop us laying this out as a vertical list, if we prefer:

When play begins:
    start the player at the Library;
    say "Welcome to Old Marston Grange, a country house cut off by fog.".

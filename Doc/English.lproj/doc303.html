21.3. Emily Short - Only the player is ever in the presence

Reported by: Emily Short / Date: 1 October 2005

Nature: The source looks legal and produces a playable game, but the game does not do what it should

Build: 2Z32

Platform: Mac OS X

Code example:

Include Locksmith by Emily Short. [Must be the latest, multi-character version.]

The Donut Shop is a room. Ogg is a transparent man in the Donut Shop. "Ogg is slumped in the corner[if Ogg carries something] with [list of things carried by Ogg]."

The Donut Shop contains a transparent closed openable locked lockable container called a case. The case contains some cake donuts. The donuts are edible. The matching key of the case is a silver key. The silver key is carried by Ogg. The mesh basket is a closed openable transparent container in the Donut Shop.

The Bagel Shop is west of the Donut Shop. A round door is east of the Donut Shop and west of the Tire Shop. The round door is a lockable locked door. The description of the round door is "It has a hole in the middle."

A persuasion rule for asking someone to try doing something:
    persuasion succeeds.

Instead of singing: say "You sing a siren song.";
    repeat with victim running through people
    begin;
        let space be the victim;
        while space is not a room
        begin;
            let space be the holder of the space;
        end while;
        let way be the best route from the space to the location, using even locked doors;
        if way is a direction
        begin;
            say line break;
            try the victim trying going way;
        end if;
    end repeat.

The dolly is pushable between rooms. It is in the Tire Shop. On the dolly is a heavy box.

The forklift is a vehicle in the Donut Shop. Igg is a woman in the forklift. The gold ball is a passkey carried by Igg. It unlocks the round door. The description is "It is perfectly round and shiny."

When play begins: start the player in the Tire Shop.

After Igg trying unlocking something with something in the presence of Ogg:
    say "'Holy crow!' bellows Ogg[if Ogg is not visible] from the other room[end if]. 'I didn't know you could do that, Igg!'";
    continue the action.

What goes wrong:

>sing

You sing a siren song.

The round door opens.

Igg arrives from the west in the forklift.

Comments:

It does work to do "when Ogg can see Igg":

>sing

You sing a siren song.

"Holy crow!" bellows Ogg from the other room. "I didn't know you could do that, Igg!"

The round door opens.

Igg arrives from the west in the forklift.

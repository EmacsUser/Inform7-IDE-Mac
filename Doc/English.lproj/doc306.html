21.6. Emily Short - Locking reported as unlocking

Reported by: Emily Short / Date: 30 September 2005

Nature: The source looks legal and produces a playable game, but the game does not do what it should

Build: 2Z20

Platform: Mac OS X

Code example:

The Sound Stage is a room. Clark Gable is a transparent man in the Sound Stage. "Clark is here, carrying [the list of things carried by Clark][if Clark wears anything] and wearing [the list of things worn by Clark][end if]." Clark carries a swagger stick. Clark carries a player's holdall called a briefcase. The briefcase contains a signed contract. [The carrying capacity of Clark is 2.] Clark wears a fedora and a pair of pants. Clark carries a portable supporter called a tray.

The stage door is west of the Sound Stage and east of the Lot. It is a door. The automobile is a vehicle in the Stage. The cage is an openable open enterable transparent container in the Stage. It is pushable between rooms.

A persuasion rule for asking Clark to try doing something: persuasion succeeds.

The player carries a small key, a tent, and Variety Magazine. The stage door is lockable and locked. The small key unlocks the stage door.

What goes wrong:

>look

[looking]

Sound Stage

Clark is here, carrying the small key, the swagger stick, the briefcase and the tray and wearing the fedora and the pair of pants.

You can also see a stage door, an automobile (empty) and a cage (empty) here.

[looking - succeeded]

>clark, unlock door

What do you want Clark Gable to unlock the stage door with?

>key

[asking Clark Gable to try unlocking stage door with small key]

[(1) Clark Gable trying unlocking stage door with small key]

Clark Gable unlocks the stage door.

[(1) Clark Gable trying unlocking stage door with small key - succeeded]

[asking Clark Gable to try unlocking stage door with small key - succeeded]

>clark, lock door with key

[asking Clark Gable to try locking stage door with small key]

[(1) Clark Gable trying locking stage door with small key]

Clark Gable unlocks the stage door. <------ **** NOT THIS TIME ****

[(1) Clark Gable trying locking stage door with small key - succeeded]

[asking Clark Gable to try locking stage door with small key - succeeded]

>open door

[opening stage door]

It seems to be locked.

[opening stage door - failed the can't open what's locked rule]

Office on Floor Fifty-One is a room. "Your office. It's cramped, what with the desk and the file drawers, but it's got a great view down Fifth Avenue."

The desk is scenery in the Office. It is a supporter. The chair is scenery in the Office. It is an enterable supporter. The file drawer is scenery in the Office. Understand "drawers" or "cabinets" or "files" or "cabinet" as the drawer. In the file drawer is a lot of paperwork. The file drawer is closed and openable. The autographed picture of Dimaggio is on the desk. The paperwork is fixed in place.

The description of the paperwork is "Copies of previously filed forms (mostly done by your predecessor), indexed by homeworld." Understand "papers" or "forms" or "form" as the paperwork.

Understand "look up [moon] in [something]" as researching in it about (with nouns reversed). Understand "consult [something] on/about [moon]" as researching in it about. Understand "read about [moon] in [something]" as researching in it about (with nouns reversed). Understand "read [moon] in [something]" as researching in it about (with nouns reversed).

Researching in it about is an action applying to one thing and one moon.

Carry out researching in it about: say "You find nothing of interest in [the noun]."

Instead of researching in paperwork about a moon listed in the Table of Paperwork Information:
    let target be the moon understood;
    if target is a moon listed in the Table of Alien Characteristics
    begin;
        choose row with moon of target in the Table of Paperwork Information;
        say "[form entry][paragraph break]";
    otherwise;
        say "You're unable to find anything -- [the moon understood] must not be an inhabited alien homeworld.";
    end if;

Instead of taking the paperwork: say "Delores will never forgive you if you get the files out of order. You could look up individual worlds, though, if you wanted."

What goes wrong:

>open file

You open the file drawer, revealing a lot of paperwork.

>read about io in paperwork

You discover nothing of interest in the lot of paperwork.

>read io in paperwork

Creature registered to Io. Distinguishing characteristics: unusual sulfuric odor, curiosity, weakness.

>look up io in paperwork

Creature registered to Io. Distinguishing characteristics: unusual sulfuric odor, curiosity, weakness.

>showverb read

Verb 'read'

* noun -> Examine
* Routine(28251) 'in' noun -> A87_researching_in_it_about reverse
* topic 'in' noun -> Consult reverse
* 'about' Routine(28251) 'in' noun -> A87_researching_in_it_about reverse
* 'about' topic 'in' noun -> Consult reverse

Comments:

Evidently the 'topic' line is scooping the 'about'... lines. I could fix this by replacing "read about..." in the after reading a command phase, but that seems hacky and inelegant, not the sort of thing we should allow young Mr Flower to see.

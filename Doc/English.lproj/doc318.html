21.4. Emily Short - Fussy parsing bug

Reported by: Emily Short / Date: 22 November 2005

Nature: The source looks legal and produces a playable game, but the game does not do what it should

Build: 3C97

Platform: Mac OS X

Code example:

Office on Floor Fifty-One is a room. "Your office[if the number of scenery things in the office is less than three]. Recent remodeling efforts have left it positively spacious, with only [the list of scenery things in the office] as permanent features[otherwise]. It's cramped, what with [the list of scenery things in the office], but it's got a great view down Fifth Avenue[end if]."

The creature is an animal in the Office on Floor Fifty-One. The description is "It's an attractive shade of [color of creature], really, when you think about it, and [if scaly]scaly[otherwise]furry[end if]."

Understand "scaly" as the creature when the creature is scaly. Understand "furry" as the creature when the creature is not scaly.

Understand the color property as describing the creature.

Color is a kind of value. The colors are red, orange, yellow, green, cyan, blue, indigo, purple, tan, black, white, and grey. The creature has a color. The creature is red.

A person has a number called metabolism. The metabolism of the player is 180. The metabolism of the creature is 2.

Definition: a person is scaly if its metabolism is 10 or less.

The cage is a container in the Office. It is openable and open.

What goes wrong:

Office on Floor Fifty-One

22.3. World model

There are missing third-party actions, to do with Clark's singing career.

Liquids are still not implemented. We have an example for ropes ("Otranto"), but still, it is conceivable that some sort of "anchoring" behaviour might be viable, and this might or might not be related to liquids.

This is big, and it is closely connected with some of the parsing issues. I would be marvelously pleased if we could acquire the functionality of zarf's demo thingy, at least as regards mixing and notional objects. I am not particularly wedded to the enumeration of objects. [ES]

Directions other than the standard cardinals cannot now be created, though they can be imitated.

Should time be more mutable, so that actions can - if they choose - specify that no time passes, after all? If so, to what extent? Do they become exempt from "after" routines? Do they count towards "... for the third turn" tallies? Emily says: if we have a check rule

"You try to pull the tag off the mattress, but it won't come."

then time should reasonably have passed for the player; whereas if instead we say

"Are you crazy? The tag says DO NOT REMOVE THIS TAG!"

the nature of the refusal is such that no time should pass. (Note: I was mostly thinking we would turn off time passing when we refused to finish actions, not when they were deemed to be successful -- that is, at check, not at carry-out. [ES])

11.3. Rules applied to activities

The activity "describing something" is the process of printing up the name of something on screen: ordinarily, this means saying the text in its "printed name" property.

As with actions, rules can be attached to activities which change or augment what would normally happen. In fact the situation is simpler, because (unlike an action) an activity always finishes, so there is no question of not reaching its "after" stage. There are also only three rulebooks attached to an activity, as compared with the six affecting an action.

The three rulebooks for describing are called "before describing", "for describing" and "after describing", and this is the general pattern. What happens is:

(i) all "before describing" rules are considered;
(ii) the most specific, applicable "rule for describing" is considered;
(iii) all "after describing" rules are considered.

The actual activity, then, is carried out by one single rule in the "for..." rulebook: it is the rule for describing whatever is concerned, hence the name.

The "before describing" and "after describing" rulebooks start out empty, while the "for describing" rulebook has just one rule in it (set to be "last" in the sense of the previous chapter): the rule which actually looks up the "printed name" property and prints it out. The same setup is used for all other activities built into Inform: nothing in "before" or "after", and the default behaviour all built into a single rule in the "for" rulebook, rigged so that any rule of the designer's will have precedence over it.

2.12. Devices and descriptions

A "device" is another of the standard kinds of thing, and should be used for anything which can be switched on or off: a light switch, say, or a slide projector. Devices are generally machines, clockwork or electrical. A device is always either "switched on" or "switched off", but is switched off unless we specify otherwise.

That makes three kinds of thing which will likely change their appearance according to which of their two possible states they are in: doors and containers, which can be open or closed; and devices, which can be switched on or switched off. We can provide for this with:

In the Undertomb is an openable container called the coffin. The coffin has description when open "The lid of a plank coffin yawns open." The coffin has description when closed "A plank coffin lies upon the dirt floor of the Tomb."

Containers and doors have the "description when open" and "description when closed" properties, whereas devices have the similar "description when on" and "description when off" properties.
